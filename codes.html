Ming's Mosaic

Made my first ever cipher! I call it "Ming's Mosaic". All it does is encrypts your text into ciphertext, keeping your message secret. Anyone who knows the key you used and has access to this code will be able to read your enocoded text. If you encrypted "Hi" with key 20, and you send your encrypted message to someone else with some clue or indication of the shift value (this can be agreed or calculated before hand). For example, if person A wanted to talk with person B, person A may send "000.1[PASTE IN CIPHERTEXT]21". Person B will then either know or work out that this message is the first message from person A (this example assumes the first three numbers identifies the person, 000, then the .1 for the first message) and that the shift person A has used is 21 (this is for the Caesar cipher). Hence person B opens up the python file, runs it, inputs the shift number, then the encoded text, and gets the secret message. Here's the link for the code!
